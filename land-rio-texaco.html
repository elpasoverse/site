Overview

Rio Texaco represents the full vision for El Paso Verse — Levels 1 through 3. The Brewster Ranch spans 353,785 acres in Brewster County, Texas, with an initial 90,000-acre partition designated as Rio Texaco, located north of Big Bend National Park.

The landscape features expansive desert plains covered in sagebrush, mesquite, and yucca, dramatic canyon formations, and mountain peaks — notably Tres Hermanos at 5,210 feet. Wildlife includes mule deer, bighorn sheep, coyotes, and hawks. Five miles of Rio Grande riverfront provide cinematic water access along the U.S.–Mexico border.

The land contains remnants of early settlements and weathered cattle trails dating to the mid-1800s, with existing buildings that can serve logistical functions during initial development phases. An additional 263,785 acres remain available through future negotiation.

Development Vision

1880s Settlement

Historically accurate frontier town construction in phased development, forming the heart of the El Paso Verse.

Expansion Infrastructure

Roads, utilities, and support systems designed for phased growth from initial settlement to full frontier territory.

Multiple Settlements

Additional towns, homesteads, camps, and outposts across the landscape for a fully immersive frontier world.

Ranching & Agriculture

Working ranching operations and agricultural support systems true to the 1880s frontier way of life.

Historic Preservation

Integration of existing early settlement remnants and cattle trails into the living frontier experience.

Note: Development of Rio Texaco is contingent on community growth in scale, reach, and PASO adoption before major implementation begins. This is the long-term vision — the ultimate destination.
